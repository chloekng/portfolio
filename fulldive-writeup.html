Building Fulldive's Community

At its core, Fulldive seeks to make virtual reality content accessible. Part of this accessibility involves presenting people relevant VR content. However, VR still feels like a gimmick to many people. Though they may find it fun to play with, it has yet to be a destination for enjoying entertainment media. Our goal was to transform Fulldive into such a content destination.

I took ownership over the user research and usability testing for this project. These insights drove how I designed for specific features on the Fulldive VR platform.

User Research

To better understand the problem space, I conducted a user study on media consumption and sharing. I conducted several in-depth interviews and sent out a survey on Online Media Practices. In total, I gathered 40-50 responses and uncovered a range of motivations for media sharing.

  • Media consumption and identity are linked for many people

  • People differ on whether they make friends based on media, or share media based on friends

  • However, people share a desire to find others who share their media interests whether this be actively (eg participating in an online community) or passively (eg observing comment sections)

  • A majority of people rely on social networks (like Facebook, Instagram, or Reddit) to provide them new media

  • 69% of people prefer sharing media via private message, in comparison to 21.4% of people who do so via public posting

  • A common motivator for sharing media is friendship/a shared interest
User Testing

We also ran a series of usability tests on the current Fulldive platform to surface prevalent pain points. These can be huge deterrants for usage on such platforms like mobile VR. Along the way, we learned many lessons about user testing for mobile VR.


Lessons in Mobile VR Testing

General Insights

  • It's easier for people to look side to side rather than up and down

  • Consider neck movements in relation to eyeline when you design

  • Don't overwhelm the user! It might be tempting to use all that space, but user's view ports are very narrow and they're in a new environment

  • Have a clearly defined hierarchy so users know where they are in the app

  • Consider distance when designing. Uncomfortably close UI is offputting. Changes in depth are an opportunity to draw attention to things

  • Clear tap targets prevent accidental misclicking, a problem especially with autoclicking

  • Smooth voice control is more important than ever in VR

  • No one likes to read, especially in VR
