Fulldive -

mobile app combining all your content in one place


Over the course of a year, I helped design a content aggregation mobile app from the ground up. I shaped a constantly evolving product vision via user research, wireframing, prototyping, user testing, and iterating.


Key screens from left to right: homepage, inside a content feed, video player, saving system


Role

  • User research, wireframing, mockups, prototyping, usability testing, interactions

  • Collaborated with Grant (Design Manager) and Anna (Product Designer)

Context


From mobile VR to content aggregation

Fulldive began primarily as a mobile VR platform. As we grew our userbase, users began requesting a mobile (flat) component to Fulldive so they could browse 360 videos even without a headset.


Around this time, Fulldive also began exploring avenues of growth beyond the virtual reality niche. With a non-VR component, we wanted to see what possibilities there were for Fulldive to evolve and draw in new users.


We evolved from a mainly mobile VR platform to a content aggregation platform.


Problem

How can Fulldive approach content curation in a unique manner that brings new value to our users?

Rethinking our product vision was a huge mission. We focused our designing around the high level question of improving users' content consumption experience.


Goals

  1. Create a mobile version of the mobile VR platform.

  2. Dig deep into what value we're providing our users, and how we can better serve our users.

  3. Build a habit-forming product.

Research

Understanding people's content consumption, curation, and sharing practices

Our target audience includes younger people who rely on their mobile phones for most of their content. I conducted 5 hour-long interviews, and sent out a survey that gathered 42 responses over a week.


I also conducted interviews with our current users to learn about their reasons for using Fulldive. Some findings:

  • People liked that Fulldive provided them access to all sorts of VR content in one space
  • The platform-wide social aspect didn't necessarily interest them, but they did express interest in finding more quality content
  • Though Fulldive provided them a good introduction to VR, they felt that after a few visits it became harder to find new, quality content

Ideation

Refining Fulldive's vision

With so many possiblities for Fulldive's future vision, we continually returned to ideating throughout the process. We had three main product directions that we kept returning to over and over:

  1. Fulldive as a place to see what content your friends are watching

  2. Fulldive as a place where you can streamline your content consumption

  3. Fulldive as a place to discover new content in their interests

Throughout our iterations, we visited different ways these product directions can integrate to create one cohesive platform.


To get a holistic view of the app, I mapped the overall information architecture of the app on our wall. This was useful in helping our team discuss together and get on the same page.


Iteration 1

Fulldive as a place to see what content your friends are watching

In our first iteration, we prioritized Fulldive as a place to see what content your friends are watching. We built a clickable prototype to test in front of users.



Iteration 1 - select mid fidelity screens; I was particularly responsible for the homepage, the content card, the menu, the profile, uploading, and bookmarks.


I facilitated 5 usability tests with people in our target demographic. We were pleasantly surprised to see that people seemed to understand the overall infrastructure of our app pretty well. However, there were some questions regarding product vision:

  • When we asked participants what problem they saw this app solving for them, they were skeptical towards the usefulness of sharing view history

  • By contrast, content aggregation stood out

  • Aggregating and customizing their feed was more compelling than seeing what their friends were watching

Iteration 2

Fulldive as a place where you can streamline your content consumption

For our second iteration, we prioritized Fulldive as a content aggregator based on people's feedback. With this iteration, we completely redesigned the homepage and introduced decks, custom feeds people can make of their favorite content sources.


Iteration 2 - select screens; I took ownership over the content cards, standardizing the article and video views, and the save/tag function.


We once again conducted usability tests. In general, we found that people grasped the product vision quicker than before and had more positive feelings towards the app.


It's pretty convenient, I have a feeling that my life will get easier. I'm pretty sure I would actually use it as it has filters to generate actual sources.

Trevor


I can see using this over reddit because while reddit has people submitting links from different sources, no one has to do any submitting and it aggregates it for you. I can just get all my sports sites in one deck and don't have to visit multiple sites, which seems pretty useful.

Shoumik

At this point, our developers completed a first iteration of the app and we were able to have limited live testing. During this testing period, we found a new set of issues we needed to improve on:

  • Some areas of the app feel disjointed from each other, like they're part of separate apps

  • Many people actually don't want that many sources per deck and would rather group their decks

  • Too many decks on the homepage makes scrolling difficult, we need a navigation system for decks

  • Content discovery is hard to find

Fulldive Mobile's third major iteration.


Iteration 3

Fulldive as a place to discover new content in their interests

For Iteration 3, we focused on improving Fulldive's capabilities for finding new content through making the search and tag system more central. We also put renewed focus on making sure the overall structure of our app made sense. On a three day design sprint, we rapidly iterated on different ways of structuring the main components of our app. In between 4 rounds of iterations, we A/B tested guerilla style on 18 people.


Iteration 3 - various iterations from our rapid iterative testing


Through this rapid iterative testing, we were able to try out many solutions from structural to language changes in a short time frame. Based on our findings, we decided to make the following high-level changes:

  • Implement bottom nav, as it lays out the main functions of the app clearly

  • Let people organize decks into "collections" accessible in the side menu

  • Let people follow tags and add them to the side menu

  • Implement a design system to unify the entire app

We're still working on implementing these changes, including flushing out more detailed flows!

We got a nice comment from Rahul. Thanks, Rahul!


Reflection

One of the biggest challenges of this project was our constantly evolving product vision. However, as we saw how users responded to each new iteration, we were able to become more confident in our direction. I also came to value the strength of a unified design team that works hard to advocate for the user. I'm looking forward to how Fulldive continues to evolve.
